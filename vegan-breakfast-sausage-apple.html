Vegan Apple Maple Breakfast Sausage

Ingredients - 1 cup Whole Grain Cooked Millet - 1 cup Cooked Lentils - 1/2 cup Date Paste - 2 tablespoons Expeller Pressed Oil - 2 tablespoons Hemp Seed - 1/2 cup Dried Apples, finely chopped - 1/4 cup Potato Starch - 2 tablespoons Ground Flaxseed - 2 tablespoons Maple Sugar - 1 teaspoon Sea Salt - 1 teaspoon Ground Fennel Seed - 1 teaspoon Thyme - 1 teaspoon Black Pepper - 1 teaspoon Sage - 1 teaspoon Smoked Paprika - 1/2 teaspoon Rosemary - 1/2 teaspoon Cinnamon - 1/4 teaspoon Nutmeg Instructions: 1. **Prepare the Base:** - In a large mixing bowl, combine the cooked millet and lentils. Mash them together using a fork or potato masher until you achieve a chunky, but cohesive mixture. 2. **Add Sweetness and Oil:** - Stir in the date paste and expeller pressed oil. Mix well to ensure the sweetness from the date paste is evenly distributed. 3. **Incorporate Dry Ingredients:** - Add the finely chopped dried apples, hemp seeds, potato starch, and ground flaxseed to the mixture. These ingredients will help bind the sausage together and add texture. 4. **Seasoning:** - Sprinkle in the maple sugar, sea salt, ground fennel seed, thyme, black pepper, sage, smoked paprika, rosemary, cinnamon, and nutmeg. Mix thoroughly to ensure the spices are evenly distributed. 5. **Form the Sausages:** - Divide the mixture into equal portions and shape them into small patties or sausage links, depending on your preference. 6. **Cooking:** - Heat a non-stick skillet over medium heat. Add a small amount of expeller pressed oil to the skillet. - Cook the sausages for about 3-4 minutes on each side, or until they are golden brown and crispy on the outside. 7. **Serve:** - Remove the sausages from the skillet and let them rest on a paper towel-lined plate to absorb any excess oil. - Serve hot with your favorite vegan breakfast sides, such as scrambled tofu, avocado toast, or a fresh fruit salad. **Tips:** - If the mixture feels too wet, add a bit more potato starch or ground flaxseed to help bind it. - These sausages can be made ahead of time and stored in the refrigerator for up to 3 days or frozen for longer storage. Reheat in a skillet before serving. Enjoy your delicious and nutritious vegan apple maple breakfast sausages!
